Location Guide: Finding High-Profit Vending Spots

A comprehensive guide to finding, evaluating, and securing the best locations for your machines.

15 High-Profit Location Types

These locations have proven high foot traffic, employee counts, and strong vending potential.

🏢 Office Buildings & Corporate Parks

Foot Traffic: Very High (100+ employees)

Best For: Snacks, drinks, combo machines

Profit Potential: $300–$800/month

  • Long operating hours (7am–6pm+)
  • Consistent daily traffic
  • Higher spending power
  • Negotiable commission with HR/facilities

🏭 Warehouses & Manufacturing Facilities

Foot Traffic: Very High (50–500 employees)

Best For: Drinks, snacks, energy products

Profit Potential: $400–$800/month

  • Captive audience on breaks
  • Limited outside food options
  • High profit margins
  • Contracts often renewable

🏫 Schools & Colleges

Foot Traffic: Very High (500+students/staff)

Best For: Snacks, drinks, healthy options

Profit Potential: $500–$1,000+/month

  • Massive foot traffic
  • Brand loyalty from young users
  • Requires school district approval
  • Breaks during holidays/summers

💪 Gyms & Fitness Centers

Foot Traffic: High (200+ member visits/day)

Best For: Healthy snacks, drinks, recovery products

Profit Potential: $300–$600/month

  • Premium pricing tolerance
  • Loyal customers
  • Pre/post-workout purchases
  • Less price-sensitive audience

💈 Barber Shops & Salons

Foot Traffic: Moderate (30–50 customers/day)

Best For: Specialty, hygiene, impulse items

Profit Potential: $100–$300/month

  • Wait time = impulse purchases
  • Captive waiting audience
  • Easy to manage
  • Premium pricing on convenience items

🔧 Auto Repair Shops

Foot Traffic: Moderate (20–50 customers/day)

Best For: Drinks, snacks, energy products

Profit Potential: $150–$350/month

  • Customers waiting for repairs
  • Hours match business hours
  • Owner often receptive
  • Steady repeat traffic

🏢 Apartment Complexes & Communities

Foot Traffic: Moderate-High (50–200 residents)

Best For: Snacks, drinks, essentials

Profit Potential: $200–$400/month

  • Captive audience (residents)
  • Common area placement
  • Long-term contracts possible
  • Maintenance agreement with management

🧺 Laundromats

Foot Traffic: Moderate (30–100 visits/day)

Best For: Snacks, drinks, urgency items

Profit Potential: $200–$400/month

  • People waiting (30–60 min)
  • Change/quarters likely on them
  • Owner often co-located
  • Regular customer base

🏨 Hotels & Motels

Foot Traffic: Moderate-High (varies)

Best For: Drinks, snacks, convenience items

Profit Potential: $300–$600/month

  • Guest convenience = high sales
  • Nights/weekends high activity
  • Corporate contract potential
  • Room service competition

⚕️ Medical Offices & Dental Clinics

Foot Traffic: Moderate (40–100 patients/day)

Best For: Healthy snacks, drinks, pain relief items

Profit Potential: $200–$400/month

  • Wait room impulse buys
  • Limited food options nearby
  • Professional environment
  • Steady patient flow

☕ Break Rooms (Corporate)

Foot Traffic: Consistent (100–300 per day)

Best For: Snacks, drinks, office supplies

Profit Potential: $400–$800/month

  • Central location in office
  • Mandatory traffic (break times)
  • Easy restocking access
  • Predictable patterns

👥 Community Centers & Recreation

Foot Traffic: High (100–400 per day)

Best For: Snacks, drinks, healthy options

Profit Potential: $300–$600/month

  • Youth and families
  • Event days = high traffic
  • Limited competitive options
  • Municipal ownership (fair terms)

🚚 Trucking Companies & Logistics

Foot Traffic: Moderate-High (50–200 per day)

Best For: Drinks, snacks, energy products

Profit Potential: $300–$600/month

  • Driver breaks = guaranteed sales
  • 24/7 or extended hours
  • Shift workers = consistent traffic
  • Premium drink pricing

🎓 Training Centers & Trade Schools

Foot Traffic: High (100–300 students/day)

Best For: Snacks, drinks, energy products

Profit Potential: $300–$600/month

  • Full-day students = high purchases
  • Limited cafeteria competition
  • Year-round (unlike schools)
  • Adult audience = higher spending

✈️ Transit & Transportation Hubs

Foot Traffic: Very High (200–1000+ per day)

Best For: Snacks, drinks, travel essentials

Profit Potential: $800–$1,500+/month

  • Massive daily traffic
  • Time-constrained customers
  • Premium pricing accepted
  • Requires vendor licensing

How to Evaluate a Location: 9 Key Criteria

Use these factors to score potential locations before committing.

1. Foot Traffic & Employee Count

Why it matters: More traffic = more sales

  • ✓ Excellent: 200+ people per day or 50+ employees
  • ✓ Good: 100–200 people per day
  • ✗ Poor: Under 50 people per day

2. Customer Wait Time & Downtime

Why it matters: Bored customers = impulse purchases

  • ✓ Excellent: 15–60 minute waits (salons, repair shops, transit)
  • ✓ Good: Break times or between activities
  • ✗ Poor: No downtime or waiting

3. Lack of Nearby Food/Drink Options

Why it matters: Less competition = more sales

  • ✓ Excellent: No nearby vending or food
  • ✓ Good: 1–2 competitors, but low quality
  • ✗ Poor: Multiple competitors, full cafeteria, food nearby

4. Hours of Operation & Access

Why it matters: Longer hours = more sales opportunities

  • ✓ Excellent: Open 7am–7pm+ (or 24/7)
  • ✓ Good: Open 8am–6pm
  • ✗ Poor: Limited hours, closed weekends/evenings

5. Security & Safety

Why it matters: Safe neighborhoods = lower theft/damage

  • ✓ Excellent: Secure building, security guards, well-maintained
  • ✓ Good: Well-lit, moderate traffic area
  • ✗ Poor: High crime area, vandalism history

6. Power Outlet Access

Why it matters: Coolers and smart machines need electricity

  • ✓ Excellent: Nearby outlet (within 6 feet)
  • ✓ Good: Outlet available with permission
  • ✗ Poor: No outlet access or expensive to install

7. Space Availability & Visibility

Why it matters: Visible, accessible placement = more sales

  • ✓ Excellent: High-traffic hallway or break room
  • ✓ Good: Visible corner, easy access
  • ✗ Poor: Hidden, cramped, hard to find

8. Owner/Manager Receptiveness

Why it matters: Cooperative partners = easier operations

  • ✓ Excellent: Owner enthusiastic, wants additional services
  • ✓ Good: Owner open, willing to try
  • ✗ Poor: Owner skeptical, difficult, resistant

9. Product Fit for Audience

Why it matters: Right products = higher margins and faster turnover

  • ✓ Excellent: Audience matches your products perfectly (gym + healthy, office + drinks)
  • ✓ Good: Audience will buy most of your products
  • ✗ Poor: Audience has different preferences (low demand)

How to Interpret Your Score

  • 80–100: 🟢 Excellent location. This is a top-tier spot. Move forward confidently.
  • 60–79: 🟡 Good location. Worth pursuing, but may need optimization.
  • 40–59: 🟠 Acceptable location. Compare with other options before deciding.
  • Below 40: 🔴 Weak location. Consider passing unless you have no other options.
