Why Start a Vending Machine Business?

✓ Low Startup Cost

Start with one machine for $3,000–$5,000. No need for employees, retail space, or complex licensing.

✓ Passive Income

Once a machine is placed and stocked, it generates revenue while you work on other things.

✓ Flexible Schedule

Manage 1–3 machines in 2–5 hours per week. Perfect for side hustles or part-time income.

✓ Scalable

Start with one machine, then scale to 5, 10, or 50+ locations. You control the growth pace.

✓ Proven Niche

Common Questions from Beginners

Most owners start between $2,500–$10,000 depending on machine type and inventory. Your first machine might cost $1,500–$3,000, with another $1,000–$3,000 for initial inventory. We break this down by starter pack on our pricing page.

Typically 7–14 days from finding a location to placing your first machine and making your first sale. Most of the time goes into location scouting and getting permission from the business owner.

It depends on your state and the products you sell. Most states require a simple business license and food handling permit. Check your local requirements—we provide a checklist in the Roadmap section.

Absolutely. Most owners manage 1–3 machines with 2–5 hours per week per machine. It's perfect for a side hustle or second income stream.

Gross margins typically range from 30–60% depending on the products and location. For example, a snack with a $0.30 cost selling for $1.00 = 70% gross margin. After restocking costs and time, net margins are typically 20–40%.

Look for high-traffic areas with employees or customers, no nearby food options, and long operating hours (offices, gyms, factories, schools). We have a full Location Guide with a scoring checklist.
