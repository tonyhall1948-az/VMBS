Outreach Tools & Scripts

Ready-to-use pitch scripts, email templates, and strategies to contact businesses and close location agreements.

30-Second In-Person Pitch Script

Use this when talking to a manager or owner face-to-face.

[Friendly, confident tone]

"Hi, my name is [Your Name]. I'm with [Your Business Name], and I provide vending machines for companies like yours. I place a [snack/drink/combo] machine in your break room or common area—completely free to you. Your employees get convenient snacks or drinks, I handle all the restocking, and we share the revenue. It's zero cost and hassle-free. Would you be open to exploring this for [location name]?"

What happens next:

  • If they say YES: "Great! I'd love to show you exactly how this works. Can we schedule 15 minutes next week?"
  • If they say NO or "Maybe": "No problem. Here's my card. Feel free to call if circumstances change."

Pitch Script Tips

  • Lead with benefit: Not "I want to put a machine here," but "Your employees will get convenient snacks and you get zero cost."
  • Emphasize zero cost: Most owners' first concern is cost. Reassure them immediately.
  • Keep it short: 30 seconds max. Don't over-explain. Leave room for questions.
  • Be friendly and professional: Dress business casual. Smile. Use their name if possible.
  • Offer next step: Don't leave hanging. Suggest a specific follow-up: "Can we schedule 15 minutes?"

Phone Pitch Script

Use this when calling a business or property manager.

Opening:

"Hi [Manager Name], this is [Your Name] with [Your Business Name]. I'm calling because we work with companies like yours to place vending machines in break rooms and common areas. Do you have about 30 seconds?"

[If they say yes, continue:]

"Perfect. So here's what we do: We place a professional vending machine with snacks [or drinks, or both] in your facility. Your employees get convenient options, and we handle everything—restocking, maintenance, even collecting the money. You don't pay anything upfront and we split the revenue with you. It's completely free to try. Would something like that make sense for [their company/location]?"

[If they're interested:]

"Excellent. I'd like to swing by and show you exactly how this works and answer any questions. Would [day/time] work for a quick 15-minute meeting?"

[If they're not interested:]

"No problem. I'll leave my contact info and if anything changes or you think of another department that might benefit, feel free to reach out. Thanks for your time!"

Phone Script Tips

  • Call during business hours (9am–3pm): Best chance to reach a decision-maker.
  • Ask for the right person: Facilities Manager, HR Manager, or Owner/Manager.
  • Keep it brief: Phone pitches should be 60 seconds or less.
  • Sound confident: Speak clearly and don't apologize for calling. You're offering a service.
  • Have a pen ready: If they say yes, you need to take down info for the site visit.
  • Call back 3-4 times if needed: Decision-makers are busy. One call often isn't enough.

Cold Email Template

Send this via email to potential locations. Personalize it with their company name and details.

Subject Line: Free Vending Machine for [Company Name]'s Employees

Email Body:

Hi [Manager Name],

I hope this finds you well. I'm reaching out because I think [Company Name] might benefit from what we do.

We specialize in placing premium vending machines in offices, break rooms, and common areas. Here's the best part: It's completely free for you. We handle 100% of the setup, stocking, and maintenance. Your employees get convenient access to quality snacks and drinks, and you share a portion of the revenue with us—no upfront cost whatsoever.

Many companies with teams like yours have seen great results. Employees love having convenient options, and our machines typically pay for themselves within the first 2–3 months.

Would you be open to a quick 15-minute conversation to see if this could work for [Company Name]? If it's not a good fit, no worries—I just wanted to explore the possibility.

Best times to reach me are [Your availability]. Feel free to call directly at [Your Phone] or reply to this email.

Looking forward to connecting!

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Your Business Name]
[Phone]
[Email]

Email Tips

  • Use a compelling subject: "Free Vending Machine" gets more opens than generic subject lines.
  • Personalize: Use their name and company name. Generic emails get deleted.
  • Lead with benefit: "Completely free" and "Your employees love it" are powerful hooks.
  • Keep it short: 150–200 words max. Respect their time.
  • Make it easy to say yes: Clear call-to-action and multiple ways to reach you.
  • Follow up: If no response in 1 week, send a gentle follow-up.

Follow-Up Message (After First Contact)

Send this 3–5 days after your first email or call if you haven't heard back.

Subject Line: Quick Follow-Up: Vending for [Company Name]

Email Body:

Hi [Manager Name],

I wanted to follow up on my email from earlier this week about our vending machine service. I know you're busy, so I'll keep this brief.

If you've already decided it's not a fit, no problem—I totally understand. But if you were interested and just haven't had time, I'd love to get on your calendar. I can work around your schedule.

Even if it's just a quick 10-minute call to explore, I think it might be worth your time. We've worked with similar companies and the feedback has been really positive.

Let me know—or feel free to call me directly at [Your Phone].

Best,
[Your Name]

How to Explain the Benefits to Business Owners

Use these talking points when pitching or explaining your service.

🎯 Benefit: Convenience for Employees

Why they care: Happy employees are more productive and loyal. Convenient perks are valued.

How to phrase it: "Your employees won't have to leave the building during breaks or lunch. They'll have quality snacks and drinks right here, which actually improves workplace satisfaction."

💰 Benefit: Zero Cost to Them

Why they care: Business owners always worry about hidden costs or obligations.

How to phrase it: "There's absolutely no cost to you upfront. I handle the machine, the inventory, the maintenance—everything. You never pay a dime. We share the revenue 70/30 [or your split], so you actually make money."

🚫 Benefit: Improved Breakroom Options

Why they care: A good breakroom improves company culture and employee retention.

How to phrase it: "Instead of employees bringing unhealthy snacks from home or leaving for fast food, they'll have quality, healthy options available. It reflects well on your company."

⚙️ Benefit: Easy Maintenance

Why they care: They don't want extra work or responsibility.

How to phrase it: "I handle everything. You never have to worry about it. I'll restock on a regular schedule, handle any technical issues, and manage all the logistics. Your team just enjoys the benefit."

📊 Benefit: Generate Extra Revenue

Why they care: Extra income is always welcome, even if modest.

How to phrase it: "Based on your office size, I estimate you'd make $100–$300/month just from this vending machine. That's pure income for minimal effort."

✅ Benefit: No Obligation or Risk

Why they care: They want an easy out if they change their mind.

How to phrase it: "Here's the thing: If it's not working after 30 days, no problem. I'll remove the machine. There's zero risk. But I'm confident your employees will love it and you'll see solid revenue."

Professional Presentation Tips

Make a great first impression in person.

Dress Code

Business casual for most locations (polo shirt or button-up, no jeans). Slightly more formal for corporate offices (dress shirt, blazer).

Communication

Be punctual, make eye contact, smile, and use their name. Listen more than you talk. Let them explain their situation first.

Materials to Bring

Business cards, a one-page brochure with your contact info, and a sample agreement or proposal if they're interested.

Handling Objections

"We already have a vending machine:" "That's great. Are your employees happy with the selection and quality? I might be able to offer something better."

"We're not interested:" "I understand. Here's my card. Feel free to reach out if circumstances change or if you think of another department."

"How much does it cost?" "Zero. You never pay anything. We share the revenue."

Closing the Deal

Always ask for a specific next step: "When can we get a machine in here?" or "What does your approval process look like?" or "Can we schedule a site inspection for [date]?"

Follow-Up

Send a thank-you email within 24 hours. Reference something specific from your conversation: "Thanks for explaining your break room setup. Here's why I think our machine would be perfect..."

Simple Agreement Terms (What to Put in Writing)

Always get a written agreement before placing a machine.

Commission or Revenue Split

Example: "Location owner receives 25% of gross revenue" or "Revenue split is 70/30 (70% to operator, 30% to location owner)"

Machine Placement Location

Specific room, floor, address. Take a photo with location coordinates marked.

Restocking Schedule

Example: "Machine will be restocked every Tuesday and Friday between 9am–5pm."

Maintenance & Support

Example: "Operator handles all maintenance, repairs, and replacement within 24 hours."

Term & Termination

Example: "Initial term is 12 months. Either party can terminate with 30 days written notice."

Access & Security

Example: "Location owner grants operator after-hours access to place and restock machine."

Payment Method

Example: "Revenue share will be paid to location owner monthly via check/ACH on the last day of the month."

⚠️ Tip: Consider having a lawyer review a template agreement before you use it (many are available online for $100–$200). It's worth the investment for peace of mind.
